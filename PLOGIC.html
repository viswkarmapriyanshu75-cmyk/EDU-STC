Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use PLUGIC without coding experience?

Yes! PLUGIC is specifically designed for non-programmers. The visual drag-and-drop interface requires no traditional coding. However, if you want to write custom blocks, basic programming knowledge helps.

What hardware does PLUGIC support?

20+ platforms: Arduino (Uno, Mega, Pro Micro), ESP8266, ESP32, micro:bit, Raspberry Pi, Adafruit boards (Arduino-compatible), and STM32 microcontrollers. Support for new boards is regularly added based on community feedback.

Can I run my logic offline on the board?

Absolutely! Compile your logic and download it to your board. It runs standalone without needing a PC or internet connection. Perfect for wearables, remote installations, and portable projects.

Is PLUGIC free and open source?

Yes! PLUGIC is released under the MIT License. All code, templates, and documentation are open source. You can fork the repository, contribute features, and even run your own instance.

How can I get help or report bugs?

Multiple options: Join our Discord for live chat support, post on the forum, or open an issue on GitHub. Response times are typically 24‚Äì48 hours. Premium support plans are also available for enterprise users.

Can I collaborate with my team on projects?

Yes! Cloud sync allows real-time collaboration. Share projects with team members, set permissions (view/edit), and maintain version history. Perfect for classrooms and professional teams.

What about data security and privacy?

Strong protection: PLUGIC uses 256-bit encryption for data in transit and at rest. We comply with GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy regulations. All code execution happens on your device or your own server‚Äîwe don't access your logic or data.

Can I integrate PLUGIC with external APIs or cloud services?

Yes! PLUGIC supports RESTful API calls, webhooks, MQTT, and custom integrations. Connect to AWS IoT, Google Cloud, Azure, ThingSpeak, and more. Our API documentation has examples for popular services.
